Profile

Dedicated Social Worker & Women's Rights Activist

With over 15 years of experience in social work, Vanita Wathore has become a beacon of hope for women facing domestic violence and social injustice in Maharashtra. Her journey began when she witnessed the struggles of women in her own community, which inspired her to dedicate her life to this cause.

Vanita's approach combines legal advocacy with emotional support, creating comprehensive solutions that address both immediate needs and long-term empowerment. She believes in creating sustainable change through education, legal reform, and community engagement.

"Every woman deserves to live with dignity and freedom. My mission is to make this a reality for as many women as possible." - Vanita Wathore

Interview

Q&A with Vanita Wathore

What inspired you to work in this field?

"I grew up witnessing the struggles of women in my community - my own aunts, neighbors, friends who suffered in silence. The turning point came when a close friend nearly lost her life to domestic violence. That's when I realized someone needed to stand up and fight for these women."

What's your approach to helping survivors?

"We take a holistic approach. Immediate safety is our first priority - getting women out of dangerous situations. Then we address legal needs, psychological trauma, and finally economic empowerment. Real change happens when a woman regains not just safety but her independence and self-worth."

What challenges do you face in this work?

"The biggest challenge is the societal mindset that still blames victims and protects abusers. We're fighting centuries of patriarchy. Another challenge is resources - there are never enough shelters, never enough funding. But every woman we help makes the struggle worthwhile."

What keeps you motivated?

"The women we've helped who come back years later as confident, independent individuals. The daughters of survivors who grow up in safe homes. The communities that transform when women are empowered. These success stories fuel my determination to keep fighting."
